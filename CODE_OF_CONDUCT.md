# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The FluxSharp project is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for all contributors, maintainers, and community members, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are dedicated to fostering a community that is:
- **Respectful** - We value diverse perspectives and treat all individuals with dignity
- **Inclusive** - We welcome contributions from developers of all skill levels and backgrounds
- **Safe** - We maintain a harassment-free environment for everyone
- **Professional** - We conduct discussions and reviews constructively and objectively
- **Collaborative** - We work together to build secure and reliable software

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Contributors are expected to:

- **Be respectful** in all communications with other community members
- **Use inclusive language** and avoid offensive terms or stereotypes
- **Give and accept constructive feedback** with grace
- **Focus on what is best** for the community and the project
- **Show empathy** towards other community members
- **Ask for help** when needed and offer assistance when you can
- **Report concerns** through appropriate channels
- **Respect intellectual property** and provide proper attribution

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable in all spaces (GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, chat, etc.):

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion, or socio-economic status
- Sustained disruption of discussions or project work
- Personal attacks or insults
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Threats of violence or violent language
- Sharing private information without explicit consent ("doxing")
- Spam or self-promotion that disrupts the community
- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns:

1. **Document the incident** - Note the date, location, individuals involved, and what occurred
2. **Report privately** - Email the project maintainers at: contact@sivagames.com
3. **Be detailed** - Provide as much context as possible to help us understand the situation
4. **Keep it confidential** - Your report will be handled with discretion

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The identity of the person making the report will be kept confidential upon request.

## Enforcement

### Consequences

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may face temporary or permanent sanctions, including:

- A warning and request to stop the behavior
- Temporary suspension from community spaces
- Permanent removal from the project and community

The project maintainers will work with the involved parties to reach a fair resolution while prioritizing the safety and well-being of the community.

### Moderation

- Maintainers have the authority to remove, edit, or reject comments, code, commits, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Maintainers may ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Appeals

If a member believes they have been unfairly treated or sanctioned, they may appeal to the project maintainers with a detailed explanation of their perspective. Appeals will be reviewed fairly and responded to within 7 days.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki)
- Email communications related to the project
- Chat channels and forums
- In-person events organized or sponsored by the project
- Any public space where an individual is representing the project
